Pose Step 1 Step 2 Step 3 Reminders
Knee-to-Chest Stretches (in bed in the morning) Lie on your back. Pull your knees up towards your face so that your thighs are resting against your chest. Reach your arms around your shins and give your legs a hug. Alternatively, if doing both legs at once doesn’t work for you, you can pull one leg at a time to your chest. 1. Put a sticky note on your alarm/phone. 2. Change your alarm text. 3. Change your alarm to a specific song that will remind you. Some silly possibilities include: Back in Black (AC/DC) I Won’t Back Down (Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers) Looking Out My Back Door (Creedence Clearwater Revival) Get Back (The Beatles) Baby Got Back (Sir Mix-A-Lot)
Back and Shoulder Stretch (when you stand up) While standing, relax your shoulders and clasp your hands behind your back. Gently pull your hands away from your back. 1. Set alarms on your phone for times when you know you’ll be sitting, like when you're watching TV in the evening. Take some extra standup sessions to help yourself get into the habit. 2. At home, put some sticky notes on doorways you frequently walk through after you stand up. At work, put a sticky note on your monitor. 3. When you’re sitting down to work, set a timer. Maybe you only have a bit of time before your next meeting. Maybe you’ve got the whole afternoon ahead of you, but it makes sense to schedule a short break in an hour. 4. Lastly, if you’ve got a kiddo at home, enlist them as your accountability buddy. Ask them to gently remind you to stretch if you stand up and start walking away without stretching.
Countertop Push-ups (while you're waiting in the kitchen) Stand in front of your kitchen counter, placing your feet together and your palms on the counter. The farther your feet are from the counter, the harder the push-up will be, so stand close to your counter! Holding your arms close to your body, bend your elbows and lower your body to the counter. Keep your back straight. Then push with your arms to raise your body back to the position in Step One. You'll need to customize your reminders based on how your routines in your kitchen work. Possibilities might include: 1. Put a note where you keep your coffee/tea/ mugs or on the kettle/coffee machine itself so you're reminded while you wait for your coffee to brew or for the kettle to boil. 2. Put a note on your stove, microwave, or toaster to remind you to do pushups while you’re boiling water for pasta or eggs, toasting bread, or microwaving your soup. 3. Put a note next to your recipe to remind yourself to do push-ups in between steps for a recipe. For example, if you’re supposed to stir your stir fry occasionally, then do a couple push-ups (instead of grabbing your phone).
